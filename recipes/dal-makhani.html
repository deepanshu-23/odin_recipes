Dal makhani

Description

Dal Makhani is a rich and creamy North Indian dish made with whole black lentils (urad dal) and kidney beans (rajma), slow-cooked with butter, cream, and aromatic spices. Here's a quick recipe you can try at home.

Ingredients

  • Whole black urad dal – 1 cup
  • Rajma (kidney beans) – 1/4 cup
  • Water – 4-5 cups
  • Butter – 2-3 tbsp
  • Cream – 1/4 cup (optional but recommended)
  • Ginger-garlic paste – 1 tbsp
  • Onion (finely chopped) – 1 medium
  • Tomato puree – 1 cup (or 2 medium tomatoes, pureed)
  • Green chili – 1 (slit or chopped, optional)
  • Kashmiri red chili powder – 1 tsp
  • Turmeric powder – 1/4 tsp
  • Garam masala – 1 tsp
  • Salt – to taste
  • Kasuri methi (crushed) – 1 tsp
  • Coriander leaves – for garnish

Steps

  1. Soak 1 cup urad dal + 1/4 cup rajma overnight (8+ hours).
  2. Rinse & Pressure cook with 4 cups water and salt for 6–8 whistles (or until soft).
  3. Heat 2 tbsp butter in a pan.
  4. Add 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste and sauté for 30 seconds.
  5. Add 1 chopped onion and cook till golden.
  6. Add 1 cup tomato puree, 1 tsp red chili powder, 1/4 tsp turmeric, salt to taste.
  7. Cook masala until oil starts separating.
  8. Add cooked dal & rajma to the masala and mix well.
  9. Add 1–2 cups water as needed for consistency.
  10. Simmer on low flame for 30–45 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  11. Add 1/4 cup fresh cream, 1 tsp garam masala, 1 tsp crushed kasuri methi, 1 tbsp butter.
  12. Simmer for another 5–10 mins.
  13. Garnish with coriander and a swirl of cream.
  14. Serve hot with naan, roti, or jeera rice.
